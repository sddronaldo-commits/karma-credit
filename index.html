About the program

What is Karma Credits?

Karma Credits is a program that celebrates students who demonstrate empathy, kindness, responsibility, and helpfulness in their surroundings.

Every helpful, kind, or responsible action earns Karma Credits — creating a culture of appreciation and encouraging more positive actions.

We believe that acknowledging empathic and positive behaviour will inspire the young ones to continue making a difference, one action at a time.

Our story

About Karma Credits

An initiative dedicated to fostering positive behaviour and building strong, empathetic school communities across India.

Our vision

Building empathy early

Empathy and inclusion create better individuals and better societies. Our vision is to nurture these qualities early through Karma (action), so young people grow up seeing kindness as strength and community as responsibility. By building Karma Credits — their social credit through everyday acts of empathy — students develop a growing record of good actions that shape their character, strengthen relationships, and contribute to a more connected and compassionate society.

Why we created this

Karma Credits is built on a simple idea — empathic, kind action matters. By recognising helpful actions, we encourage students to turn kindness, responsibility, and collaboration into everyday habits. When young people feel seen for doing good, they're more likely to support others and grow into compassionate, responsible individuals who strengthen their communities.

Our Objectives

◆Help create more empathetic, proactive citizens
◆Motivate positive social behaviour through recognition and reinforcement
◆Build social credit through empathy-driven experiences
◆Increase inclusiveness and social awareness
◆Build a network of socially responsible student communities
Got questions?

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about the Karma Credits program.

Karma Credits are points earned by students aged 11–18 when they perform empathic, helpful, or responsible actions. Each action can be submitted through our app and verified, after which credits are added to the student's account that can be redeemed for rewards.
Students between 11–18 years of age can participate. Schools, learning spaces, parents, and community partners can also join as supporting members to help recognize and reward student actions.
Any genuinely helpful, kind, or responsible action counts — helping a classmate, volunteering in the community, supporting someone who is upset, picking up litter, assisting an elderly person, or any other empathic act demonstrating care for others.
Students submit their action through the app with a photo or video and a short description. Actions are reviewed by school coordinators or community partners, after which credits are assigned based on the nature and impact of the action.
Rewards vary by partner and school — certificates, gift vouchers, experiences, recognition at events, or tangible items. The reward catalogue is updated regularly by Karma Credits and its community partners.
Yes, entirely voluntary. Karma Credits encourages positive behaviour through motivation and recognition, not obligation. Students, parents, and schools choose to participate because they believe in the mission.
We take student privacy very seriously. All data is stored securely and we comply with data protection norms. Photos and videos are only accessible to authorised school coordinators and Karma Credits administrators.
Students can use a parent's phone, a school device, or submit through a teacher or parent on their behalf. We are committed to making the program accessible to all students.
No cap at all. The more good actions you take, the more credits you accumulate. We celebrate every act of kindness, big or small.
Karma Credits helps you build a record of positive contributions that reflects your character and values — beyond academics. It builds empathy, social responsibility, and community belonging, while giving you tangible recognition and rewards for the good you already do.
